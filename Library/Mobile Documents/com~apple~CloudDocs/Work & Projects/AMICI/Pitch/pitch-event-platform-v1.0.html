The Problem

"Can you organize something
nice for 40 people?"

Every year, thousands of Swedish companies plan team dinners, client events, kickoffs, and holiday parties. The task lands on office managers, HR leads, and assistants who have no event experience, no supplier network, and no idea what things should cost. That knowledge gap turns a straightforward booking into weeks of wasted time, scattered coordination, and overspending.

Business Model

Commission today. Platform tomorrow.

Now
20%
supplier-side commission

Suppliers pay a 20% commission on bookings. Customers pay a small service fee on top. Think Airbnb: both sides contribute, but the total cost stays well below the 20-35% agencies charge on the entire event. Transparent pricing, no hidden markups. ~50K SEK/month booking volume.

Next
Multi-service
bundled commissions

As we add supplier categories, revenue per event grows. One booking can include chef + venue + transport + equipment.

Later
Premium + SaaS
concierge & supplier tools

Concierge tier: clients pay Amici to manage events end-to-end. Supplier SaaS: featured listings, analytics, priority matching. Plus supplier kickbacks on volume.

Why can't single platforms just add more services? Multi-service coordination is an entirely different product. Venuu knows venues. Take a Chef knows chefs. Neither has the cross-category supplier relationships or the orchestration layer to tie it all together. Amici works like Airbnb: suppliers pay a commission, customers pay a small service fee. The combined cost to the customer stays a fraction of what any agency would charge.
